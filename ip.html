Wood Portfolio Book

In my Junior year I had joined an after school course called MakerLab. In this class we worked on creating portfolio books from wood for ourselves. We first started with coming up with our design for the front cover. We each had to take inspiration from nature and I chose a close up image of a leaf. Afterwards we traced over our sketches with micron markers, we imported a picture onto Illustrator to fix any line issues (basically any lines that were not connected and were going nowhere were causing issues in what the cutter deems should be lazered) in the sketch. After fixing the sketches in Illustrator we got to laser cutting. Below is the nature pattern I chose and my sketch. I chose to actually etch my design onto the wood instead of cutting it because I felt that would be better for my design.

Wood Portfolio Book V2

This was my most recent project for my IDA class. The project required us to either create a portfolio book or a box with our designs cut from wood. I chose to make another portfolio book as i felt that i could come up with a much better design than my previous one. My design took inspiration from a city grid system with each grid representing a building or other structure. I had printed two layers of my design the first layer was the initial design and the second layer was an offset of the first layer .I then glued the two layers together and ended up with my final product.

Second Custom Built Computer

Last year I decided to build a second computer, this time for my dad who's pc had started to age and slow down. I figured that since I already built one for myself I can do one for him too. Since he mainly uses his pc for checking emails, browsing the web etc I figured that I wouldnt need to buy expensive and more modern parts. So I went with:
  • Ryzen 3 with intergrated graphics
  • Msi Bazooka micro atx motherboard
  • 500W psu
  • 500gb nvme ssd and a 1tb HDD
  • 8gb ram

First Custom Built Computer

Towards the middle of freshman year of high school my school went full virtual. The only device that I had at the time to use for school was the school issued chromebook, which had a lot of problems. I was able to stand it for a few months before I hit a breaking point and decided that I needed something better. I thought about maybe getting a new laptop but I figured that I at least have the chromebook for portability, even though its not the best. For some time the idea of building a pc had been stuck in my head and I realized that now was the time. After hours and days of researching and ordering parts and then spending a whole day assembling it, the pc was complete. This was one of the most important projects for me and a very personal one too that I look back at often. For the parts I went with:
  • Ryzen 5 CPU
  • B450 DS3H micro atx motherboard
  • rx 570 gpu
  • 600W psu
  • 1 tb hdd and 500gb ssd
  • 16gb ram
